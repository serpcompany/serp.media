## Buyer's Guide

Fluance record players offer a unique blend of performance and affordability. Whether you're a first-time record collector or a seasoned audiophile, understanding the key features and considerations will help you find the perfect turntable to suit your needs. 



### Important Features

1. Type of Turntable: Fluance record players come in both manual and automatic versions. Manual turntables require you to drop the needle yourself, while automatic turntables use an electronic sensor to automatically drop the needle. Automatic turntables are generally more beginner-friendly. 


### Considerations

1. Speakers: If you're just starting, consider purchasing a record player with built-in speakers. This saves you the hassle of setting up external speakers. As you become more experienced, you may want to consider investing in a higher-quality external speaker setup. 

2. Sound Quality: Look for features like high-torque motors, adjustable pitch control, and thick, heavy plinths to ensure better sound quality. A thick, heavy plinth helps reduce vibrations and provides better stability. 

3. Speed Adjustment: Turntables typically have three different speeds: 33 1/3, 45, and 78 RPM. Ensure the turntable you choose has the correct speed for your records. 



### General Advice

1. Maintenance: Proper maintenance is essential to ensure your record player lasts for years. Clean your records regularly and handle them carefully to prevent scratches. Clean your stylus regularly to ensure optimal sound quality. 

2. Compatibility: Check the compatibility of your turntable with the records you have or plan to collect. Most record players can play both 33 1/3 and 45 RPM records. However, there are some that can play 78 RPM records as well. 

3. User-friendly design: Look for a turntable with an easy-to-use control panel. Buttons for speed adjustment, on/off, and start/stop should be clearly labeled and accessible. 

Choosing the right Fluance record player involves considering the type of turntable, the quality of the built-in speakers (if any), the speed adjustment, and the overall design. With these factors in mind, finding the perfect turntable for your collection should be a breeze.
