### Turntable Size and Speed


There are two main types of turntables: the 7-inch (18 cm) and the 12-inch (30.5 cm). The size of the turntable affects the speed at which it can play records. The most common recording speed is 33 1/3 rpm (revolutions per minute) for 12-inch records and 45 rpm for 7-inch records. Make sure the Garrard Record Player you choose is compatible with the records you intend to play. 


### Stylus and Needle Quality

The stylus and needle are critical components of a record player, as they interact directly with the vinyl records. High-quality needles are necessary to avoid any damage to the records and ensure a clear, accurate sound. Look for a Garrard Record Player with a replaceable stylus made of high-quality materials, such as diamond or sapphire.

## FAQ



### What is a Garrard Record Player?

Garrard Record Players are a series of high-quality turntables designed for vinyl enthusiasts who value exceptional sound quality and build. Garrard has been producing record players since the 1920s, and their products are known for their exceptional craftsmanship and performance. 


### How do I set up a Garrard Record Player?

To set up a Garrard Record Player, you will need to connect it to a power source, ensure that the record is placed on the platter, lower the tonearm onto the record, and adjust the tracking force and azimuth for optimal performance. Consult the product's user manual for specific instructions. 


### What are some common issues with Garrard Record Players?


* Worn or damaged components, which can affect sound quality and cause performance issues.

* Some models may require periodic maintenance and lubrication to maintain optimal performance.

* The tonearm may need adjustment, particularly if there are noticeable vibrations during playback.

To address these issues, it's essential to follow the manufacturer's recommendations for care and maintenance and consult a professional if necessary. 


### What are some features to look for in a Garrard Record Player?

* High-quality construction materials, such as cast aluminum or steel, for durability and weight stability.

* Adjustable features, such as the tonearm angle and azimuth, for optimal tracking and sound quality.

* A well-designed platter and bearing system to minimize friction and noise.

These features contribute to a Garrard Record Player's overall performance and reputation for exceptional sound quality. 


### Do Garrard Record Players support different types of records?

Yes, Garrard Record Players are compatible with various record formats, including 12-inch, 10-inch, and 7-inch records. Always ensure that the turntable is configured correctly for the chosen record size and speed. 


### Can Garrard Record Players be upgraded or modified?

Yes, many Garrard record players can be upgraded or modified by swapping out components, such as the cartridge or tonearm, to improve performance and sound quality. Always consult the product manual or a professional for guidance on such upgrades. 


### How should I store a Garrard Record Player when not in use?

When not in use, store your Garrard Record Player in a dry, clean area and cover it to protect from dust and debris. If possible, remove the tonearm and place the stylus on the arm rest to prevent damage. Do not expose the turntable to extreme temperatures or humidity, as this can affect its performance and longevity.
